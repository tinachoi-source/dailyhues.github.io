RED

Start by walking toward the sun. In the morning, that means heading east, letting the first light spill across the streets, and in the late afternoon, turn west to catch the golden glow. As you walk, let your eyes wander naturally, scanning everything around you. Look down at the ground and notice the little details: fallen leaves, tiny berries hiding among the grass, or a bright splash of color on a curb. Lift your gaze to the world around you. Notice people passing by in jackets, hats, or shoes that catch the sunlight with flashes of red. Keep your eyes moving even higher, toward street signs, traffic lights, and flags fluttering above, and watch for birds perched in the trees or insects with unexpected red markings. Red can appear in the most surprising places, a flower tucked between branches, the gleam of a parked car, or a mailbox standing boldly by the sidewalk. Let your walk feel like a treasure hunt, each red spot a small prize waiting to be discovered. Pause occasionally, take it all in, and see how many reds you can spot. The more you look, the more the world seems to reveal its hidden bursts of color, turning an ordinary walk into a quiet, joyful adventure.
